You see, I have a greater ambition than helping
you create this atlas.
Several years ago, I coded a n-d audiovisual gallery with captions.
I had something else in mind, and never considered using it as an Atlas.
Anyway, I shelved it in favor of other stuff, like Talking Ringtones.
Then you came along. Eureka! A good way to use 2-d!
So now I'm using this work with you to climb a learning curve
on how to create and publish a small set of programs that
make it as easy as possible for anybody to create a local atlas
of trees, birds, wild flowers, textiles, native masks of Bali, Noro viruses,
anything they can systematize and want to share.

The difficult part is publication. 
How do you deliver your altlas to those interested?
How do you get the world to notice your atlas?
The best answer, I suppose, is volume:
If there were many atlases, for all kinds of subjects,
then atlases would become a part of people's consciousness.
When the Smiths go to Yellowstone, they naturally think: 
let's get the atlases for the trees, wildflowers, geologic stuff, ...
It would become worthwhile for various actors
to create distribution channels, even marketplaces.
